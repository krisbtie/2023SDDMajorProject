Population facts in 2021 census

According to the 2021 Australian census, the country's population has continued to grow steadily, reaching 25.7 million people, an increase of around 1.7 million since the last census in 2016. The census data shows that the most populous states in Australia are New South Wales, Victoria, and Queensland, which together account for almost 80% of the population.

Population by Generation

The 2021 Australian census reveals that the Baby Boomer generation, born between 1946 and 1965, remains one of largest generations in the country, alongside Millennials, born between 1981 and 1996, both of which comprise approximately 21.5% of the population each. The following largest generations are Gen X, born between 1966 and 1980, accounting for around 19.3% of the population, and Gen Z, born between 1997 and 2012, representing around 18.2%. Gen Alpha, born 2012 and onwards, then makes up 12% and Interwar, born 1946 and before, makes up 7.5%.

Population by State

The 2021 Australian census data reveals that New South Wales is the most populous state with over 8 million residents, followed by Victoria and Queensland. While the three eastern states are the most populous, the data also highlights the significant differences in population size between states and territories.

Population by Age Group, Male, Female

The 2021 Australian census data reveals that New South Wales is the most populous state for both males and females, followed by Victoria and Queensland.
